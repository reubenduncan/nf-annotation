Interpretation

Integrated analysis of all six pipelines for Serratia fonticola DSM 4576 in the context of the Serratia genus.

Summary: S. fonticola DSM 4576 is an environmentally stable, entirely chromosomal genome with a unique β-lactamase complement (blaSFDC + blaFONA-1) shared only with S. silvae. Despite carrying no plasmids, integrons, or transferable resistance clusters, the genome is peppered with 34 insertion sequences representing a significant source of genomic plasticity. Its CARD resistome is broad but dominated by intrinsic chromosomal efflux machinery rather than acquired elements, consistent with its non-clinical, freshwater origin.

🧬 Genome Architecture

  • Single circular chromosome of 6.00 Mb (53.6% GC) — no plasmids confirmed by both MOB-Suite and PlasmidFinder
  • Absence of integrons means no integron-mediated cassette capture; resistance genes cannot be rapidly reshuffled via this route
  • Genome is a closed, complete reference assembly (NZ_CP011254.1), providing high confidence in gene-absence calls

💊 β-Lactam Resistance

  • blaSFDC encodes an extended-spectrum class C β-lactamase (AmpC-type), conferring intrinsic cephalosporin resistance. At 97.1% identity it is a native chromosomal gene, not a recent acquisition
  • blaFONA-1 is a class A β-lactamase with 100% identity to the reference — the defining resistance gene of S. fonticola, reported since the 1980s
  • The blaSFDC + blaFONA-1 pairing is unique to fonticola/silvae, distinguishing them from the SRT-dominated clade (marcescens, bockelmannii, nevei, etc.)

🔀 Mobile Genetic Elements

  • 34 IS elements from 7 families distributed across the full 6 Mb chromosome (~1 per 176 kb)
  • IS481 (×8) and IS110 (×8) dominate; their multicopy nature raises the probability of IS-mediated recombination and gene disruption events
  • IS200/IS605 elements (×6) are known to transpose without host machinery — notable for cryptic mobility
  • Most elements are complete (type 'c', 76%), indicating active or recently active transposons

🎯 Broader Resistome (CARD RGI)

  • 11 of 19 hits are efflux pumps spanning RND, MFS, ABC, and SMR families — characteristic of intrinsic, chromosomally-encoded multidrug efflux
  • vanG/vanW glycopeptide resistance genes are noteworthy but likely represent a chromosomal van cluster rather than acquired vancomycin resistance (low-level intrinsic)
  • FosA8 fosfomycin thiol transferase and EF-Tu mutations (elfamycin) are unusual findings that warrant experimental validation
  • PBP3 alteration suggests reduced susceptibility to some β-lactams independently of β-lactamase activity

📊 Genus Context

  • 44% of Serratia species carry no core AMR genes by AMRFinder — including the environmental species aquatilis, entomophila, ficaria, grimesii, etc.
  • The "SRT clade" (bockelmannii, marcescens, nevei, nematodiphila, surfactantfaciens, ureilytica) consistently carry blaSRT + tet(41) + aac(6') — a conserved three-gene intrinsic resistance cluster
  • S. fonticola diverges from this clade entirely, carrying distinct class A + class C β-lactamases with no tetracycline or aminoglycoside genes
  • S. symbiotica (insect endosymbiont) carries only a qac gene, reflecting its reduced/adapted genome

⚠️ Clinical & Ecological Implications

  • S. fonticola is rarely clinical but has been isolated from immunocompromised patients — its dual β-lactamase profile means penicillin and cephalosporin monotherapy would likely fail
  • No transferable resistance elements (plasmids, integrons) detected, limiting HGT risk to IS-mediated events
  • The high IS element load (34 copies) indicates genomic instability potential, which could drive adaptation under antibiotic selection
  • Freshwater environmental reservoir role: monitoring water sources for S. fonticola may be warranted in clinical catchment areas
